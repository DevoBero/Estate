Incorporating a raised flower bed in your yard allows your blossoms to take center stage as the main focal point, since they stretch taller than the surrounding plant life. Interlocking rock walls form a unique aesthetic, based on the stone sizes and hues.

Unlike concrete block walls, you do not need to add rebar or other supportive posts into the interlocking rock wall. Rebar use usually requires pouring concrete into your wall structure to hold the metal posts. The interlocking strategy uses a mixture of large, medium and small rocks to form a structural mesh so that concrete and rebar are not necessary.
